Origins

My work is based on the old world art of Pyrography. A style of art that decorates wood using burn marks from a heated object. The Art style has been around as early as the discovery of fire and has been utilized and practiced in a number of different cultures and countries throughout history including the Egyptians, some African tribes, Romania, Hungary, and Flanders as well as Argentina and other areas in South America. It was also used during the Han Dynasty in China where it was known as Fire Needle Embroidery. The Greeks used the term “pur” which means fire and used the term “graphos” meaning writing to describe the craftwork.

Types of Art Sytles

  • Abstract - usually about shape, line, value, color, and/or texture.
  • Art Nouveau - illusionistic but primarily flat, that are highly pattern driven, and that usually incorporate fluid, curving lines.
  • Manga - Japanese comic book style developed in 19th Century Japan.
  • Post-Impressionism - drawings based on light (the preoccupation of the impressionists) but with more attention to geometric shapes.
  • Realism - convincing representations of reality.
  • Surrealism - dreamlike and sometimes startling works based on pure imagination.
